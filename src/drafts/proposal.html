Project Proposal: Partner Matching Web Application

Problem Domain

People often need to connect with others for various purposes, such as finding study partners, carpooling for weekend ski trips, or matching mentors with students. In these situations, users are typically looking for individuals who share similar interests and have compatible schedules. However, existing platforms often make this process inefficient and frustrating.

Current solutions such as social media groups or event-based platforms tend to have scattered information, unclear organization, and require extensive manual searching. As a result, users may struggle to find suitable matches, leading to low engagement, poor retention, and missed opportunities for meaningful connections.

This web application is hoping to address these challenges by providing a simple, interactive system that matches users based on their interests and availability. While similar platforms already are present, many matching processes remain fragmented, disorganized, or underutilized. By focusing on user-generated data and streamlined interactions, this project seeks to improve accessibility, efficiency, and user engagement in partner matching.

Existing Solutions

  • Meetup – A popular platform for discovering interest-based events and groups, but often requires manual searching and does not provide personalized matching.
  • Facebook Groups – Social media-based groups that connect people with similar interests, though information is often scattered and difficult to organize efficiently.

App Description

This web application is designed to link individuals through common interests and schedules, enabling the formation of small, purpose-driven relationships. Consider study groups, carpools, hobby meetups, or mentor-mentee pairings. The app is aimed at students, young professionals, and community members looking for focused connections, away from the noise of conventional social media. By emphasizing structured matching rather than open-ended feeds, the app aims to cultivate more equitable and intentional connections, particularly for those who may feel overlooked or overwhelmed by existing social networks.

The application is built around user-generated profile and availability information, and it will offer these interactive features: Users will build their profiles by filling out interactive forms with their interests—like studying, sports, or travel—and what they're hoping to achieve. They'll also indicate their weekly availability. This data is stored for the long haul, forming the backbone of the app's functionality. Users can then browse potential matches, using dynamic filters to find people with shared interests, compatible schedules, and the right kind of connection in mind, whether that's a study partner or someone to share a carpool with. Filters instantly refresh the results as you type, letting you quickly sift through and find the most pertinent matches.

Users can save or bookmark promising matches in a personal list, perhaps labeled "Interested" or "Contact Later." This gives saved connections a purpose, allowing users to organize and revisit potential contacts whenever they want. These features work together as a unified system: users input structured data, the app processes and organizes that data to facilitate matching, and users can manage their potential connections over time. By minimizing the need for unstructured posts and manual searches, the app tackles the shortcomings of platforms like Meetup and Facebook Groups, offering a focused, user-friendly approach to building meaningful connections.

App Description

This web application links individuals through common interests and schedules, enabling small, purpose-driven relationships such as study groups, carpools, hobby meetups, or mentor-mentee pairings. The app targets students, young professionals, and community members seeking focused connections without the noise of conventional social media. Structured matching can be especially helpful for newcomers, introverted users, and people from minority groups who may feel overlooked in large online spaces.

  • Profile & Availability Creation (Data Collection Feature): Users create profiles with interests, goals, and weekly availability through interactive forms. This information is saved and reused across sessions.
  • Partner Matching & Filtering (Data Organization Feature): Users browse potential matches and apply dynamic filters based on shared interests, availability overlap, and connection type.
  • Connection Tracking (Small Feature): Users can bookmark promising matches into a personal list (e.g., “Interested” or “Contact Later”) for future reference.

Together, these features form a unified system in which users contribute structured data, the application organizes that data to surface compatible matches, and users manage potential connections over time using persistent storage. This approach directly addresses the inefficiencies of platforms like Meetup and Facebook Groups.
